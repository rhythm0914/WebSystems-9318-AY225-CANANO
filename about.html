About Bacoor City

Learn about our history, vision, mission, and leadership

Our History

Bacoor, officially the City of Bacoor, is a 1st class component city in the province of Cavite, Philippines. According to the 2020 census, it has a population of 664,625 people, making it the second most populous city in Cavite after Dasmariñas.

Bacoor was established as a pueblo (town) in 1671. It became a city on June 23, 2012, after the ratification of Republic Act No. 10160. The city is known as the "Gateway to the Province of Cavite" due to its strategic location.

Historically significant, Bacoor played a crucial role during the Philippine Revolution. It was the site of the Battle of Zapote Bridge in 1897 and served as the first capital of the Revolutionary Government under Gen. Emilio Aguinaldo.

To provide excellent public service through responsive governance, sustainable development, and inclusive growth that enhances the quality of life of every Bacoorian.

Our Core Values
